Database Organization - ERDs

TL;DR

What: Demonstrate simple relationship diagrams for databases I created

Why ERDs?

ERD stands for Entity Relationship Diagram and I am a big fan of using them anytime I am beginning a new project that utilizes a database. ERDs are basically a snapshot of the entities(tables/objects) in your database and how they are related to other tables/objects. There are different types of ERDs. What you will be seeing in this demo are more conceptual diagrams. The reason I am more keen to conceptual diagrams is because I feel like they are high level enough for customers/product owners to understand, yet a developer can easily translate them into actual tables/objects.

ERDs, in my opinion, should be some of the first things you create when planning for you database. Why? Because they explicitly depict how the database can and will be organized. Showing different relationships will make setup go loads quicker.

Just as before, the entities are clearly exposed, the relationships are seen and can be easily translated, and the properties of each object are organized and precise. The "M" and "N" in both of these diagrams represent a many to many relationship. e.g. MetricQuery can have many different Connections and a Connection can have many different MetricQuerys that are referring to it.

Final Remarks

Getting in the habit of making ERDs before jumping into table creation and adding rows, has really helped me in the long run. Taking the time to visualize how I want my data to be stored, what types of properties my entities will have, what type of relationships do I want these entities to have will greatly reduce the amount of rework and coding that will need to be done. Not only that, but it will help ensure that your database follows relational patterns.
