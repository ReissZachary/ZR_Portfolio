Tinker Gems Jewelry

TL;DR

What: A website for sisters jewelry company

Why: To help ease process of receiving and filling orders and to get her company name out there

How: Built with .NET Core Razor pages, HTML, CSS, and takes advantage of REST API endpoints

Summary

Tinker Jems Jewelry is a small local jewelry shop owned by my sister. She makes many different types of jewelry and sells them as a hobby. As of recent, she has been receiving more and more requests for her jewelry. This led my sister t0 approach me with an idea to help her small local business grow and to help facilitate the process of ordering and recieving her handmade jewelry. She asked if I could make a website that could still encapsulate her 'local' feel and keep the customer and owner relationship strong. She wanted something simple, yet complete. She wanted to keep the communication between her and the customer as much as she could. While there are multiple components in her webpage, I want to dicuss the use of the API's.

The API

I had two choices to make regarding API's. I was choosing between RESTful and SOAP API's. I ended up choosing REST. There were a few main reasons I went down the RESTful path:

  • I was aware that SOAP uses XML and REST uses JSON. From what I know, I can have a greater variety of data formats with JSON. Hence, making it easier to work with for me
  • JSON has better support for browser clients. After all, it is highly used by Yahoo, ebay, Amazon, and even Google
  • REST has superior performance especially when it comes to caching for information.

Final Remarks

CURRENT STATUS: This website is up and running on Heroku here. She is still in the process of adding all her jewelry items to the page and I am still working on implementing some sort of checkout process. Due to the recent COVID-19 pandemic, things have been halted for the time being.

Some other really cool features of this site include:

  • Has admin role so that my sister can log in and add/update/remove jewelry items along with any pictures or tags
  • There is blog capabilities for my sister to create posts about current jewelry deal/inspirations/stories.
  • Each jewelry item can have many tags. Each tag can be associated with many different jewelry items. This allows users to click on tags to get a page with all the items with similar tags!
